This Cookie Policy explains how tranzyNote ("we", "us", "our") uses cookies and similar tracking technologies when you visit our website and use our services. This policy should be read in conjunction with our Privacy Policy.

1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your computer or mobile device when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently, provide a better user experience, and gather information about website usage.

1.1 Types of Technologies

In addition to cookies, we may use similar technologies such as:

  • Web Beacons: Small graphic images embedded in web pages or emails to track user activity
  • Local Storage: Technology that allows websites to store data locally in your browser
  • Session Storage: Temporary storage that is cleared when you close your browser
  • Pixels: Tiny graphics used to track user behavior and conversions

2. How We Use Cookies

We use cookies and similar technologies for the following purposes:

  • Essential Functions: To enable core website functionality and secure user authentication
  • Security: To protect against fraudulent activity and enhance platform security
  • Performance Monitoring: To analyze how our website and services are performing
  • User Preferences: To remember your settings, preferences, and login state
  • Analytics: To understand how visitors interact with our website and improve user experience
  • Service Delivery: To provide the tranzyNote desktop application and meeting assistant features

Important Note: We do not use cookies for advertising purposes or to track you across other websites. Our cookies are strictly used to improve your tranzyNote experience and provide our services.

3. Types of Cookies We Use

We use the following categories of cookies:

Cookie Type Purpose Duration
Strictly Necessary Required for the website to function. Enable core features like security, login, and account access. Cannot be disabled. Session / Persistent
Performance Help us understand how visitors use our site by collecting anonymous usage statistics and performance data. Up to 2 years
Functional Remember your preferences, settings, and choices to provide an enhanced and personalized experience. Up to 1 year

3.1 Session vs. Persistent Cookies

  • Session Cookies: Temporary cookies that are deleted when you close your browser. These are essential for maintaining your login session.
  • Persistent Cookies: Remain on your device for a set period or until you delete them. These help us remember your preferences across visits.

5. Managing Cookie Preferences

5.1 Browser Controls

Most web browsers allow you to control cookies through their settings. You can:

  • View what cookies are stored on your device
  • Delete all cookies or specific cookies
  • Block all cookies or cookies from specific websites
  • Set your browser to notify you when a cookie is set

5.2 How to Disable Cookies

You can disable cookies by adjusting your browser settings. Here's how for popular browsers:

  • Chrome: Settings > Privacy and security > Cookies and other site data
  • Firefox: Options > Privacy & Security > Cookies and Site Data
  • Safari: Preferences > Privacy > Cookies and website data
  • Edge: Settings > Cookies and site permissions > Cookies and site data

Important: If you disable cookies, some features of tranzyNote may not function properly. Strictly necessary cookies are required for the website to work and cannot be disabled without affecting functionality.

5.3 Do Not Track

Some browsers include a "Do Not Track" (DNT) feature that signals to websites that you do not want your online activity tracked. We respect DNT signals and will not track users who have DNT enabled, except for essential cookies required for website functionality.

5.4 Mobile Devices

For mobile devices, you can manage cookie preferences through your device settings or browser app settings. Refer to your device manufacturer's instructions for specific guidance.
